Three English proficiency tests dominate university admissions. They serve the same purpose but differ in format, acceptance patterns, and suitability for different test-takers.

## Quick Comparison

- **Test length**: 2h45min · 2h · 2h
- **Cost (2026)**: ~A$410 · ~A$350 · ~A$375
- **Scoring**: 0–9 (band scores) · 0–120 (total) · 10–90 (overall)
- **Results**: 13 days · 4–8 days · 48 hours
- **Valid for**: 2 years · 2 years · 2 years
- **Exam format**: Paper or computer · Computer only · Computer only


## University Requirements (typical, check specific university)

- **Australian universities**: 6.5 (no band <6.0) · 79–90 · 58–64
- **UK universities**: 6.0–7.0 · 80–100 · 55–70
- **US universities**: 6.5–7.5 · 80–100 · 58–70
- **Canadian universities**: 6.5–7.0 · 83–100 · 60–70


## Which Test Should You Take?

**Choose IELTS if**: You're applying to Australia, UK, or Canada — IELTS is the most widely accepted. The paper option benefits those who don't type quickly.

**Choose TOEFL if**: You're applying to the US — TOEFL is the most widely accepted there. The integrated tasks (reading + listening + speaking) suit academic English preparation.

**Choose PTE if**: You need results fast (48 hours), prefer AI-scored speaking (no human examiner anxiety), or IELTS Speaking gives you anxiety (PTE speaking is to a computer, not a person).

One under-appreciated factor: **test centre availability**. In many countries, IELTS test centres are more numerous than TOEFL or PTE centres. Check what's available in your city before choosing.
