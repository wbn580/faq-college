International tuition fees vary dramatically by country, university, and discipline. This comparison provides typical ranges for undergraduate international students in English-speaking destinations and key European alternatives.

## Annual Tuition Fees (AUD equivalent, 2026)

| Country | Arts/Humanities | Business/Law | Science/Engineering | Medicine |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Australia | $36,000–45,000 | $40,000–50,000 | $45,000–52,000 | $75,000–85,000 |
| UK | $30,000–42,000 | $35,000–55,000 | $38,000–58,000 | $50,000–80,000 |
| US (public) | $35,000–50,000 | $35,000–55,000 | $40,000–55,000 | $55,000–75,000 |
| US (private) | $60,000–80,000 | $60,000–85,000 | $65,000–85,000 | $70,000–90,000 |
| Canada | $25,000–40,000 | $30,000–45,000 | $35,000–50,000 | $50,000–75,000 |
| Netherlands | $15,000–25,000 | $15,000–25,000 | $15,000–25,000 | $30,000–40,000 |
| Germany (public) | $0–3,000 | $0–3,000 | $0–3,000 | $0–3,000 |

## Total Cost of a 3-Year Degree

All figures include tuition + living costs, AUD:

- **Australia (3 years)**: $170,000–$240,000
- **UK (3 years)**: $150,000–$240,000
- **US private (4 years)**: $320,000–$400,000
- **US public (4 years)**: $200,000–$280,000
- **Canada (4 years)**: $160,000–$230,000
- **Netherlands (3 years)**: $90,000–$130,000
- **Germany (3 years)**: $40,000–$60,000 (nearly all living costs)

## Hidden Costs

- **Health insurance**: OSHC (Australia $600–800/year), NHS surcharge (UK $900/year), provincial health (Canada varies)
- **Student services fees**: $300–500/year at most universities
- **Flights home**: Budget $1,500–3,000/year for one round trip
- **Visa renewal costs**: Budget $500–1,000 for the duration of study

Germany offers the best value for cost-conscious students — some states charge only a semester contribution (€200–400/semester) and living costs are moderate. The trade-off: most undergraduate programs are German-taught.
