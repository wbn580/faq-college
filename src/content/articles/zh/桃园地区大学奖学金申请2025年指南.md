## Municipal Government Scholarships: Taoyuan City Scholarship Program

The **Taoyuan City Scholarship Program** is the largest single source of local funding, offering NT$80 million in total awards for the 2025-2026 cycle. This program targets students who have resided in Taoyuan for at least one year and maintain a minimum GPA of 3.0 on a 4.0 scale.

### Eligibility and Award Tiers
Undergraduate recipients receive NT$20,000 per semester, while graduate students qualify for NT$30,000 per semester. The program requires full-time enrollment at a Taoyuan-based university. In 2024, 3,200 students received awards, and the city projects 3,500 recipients for 2025.

### Application Timeline
Applications open March 1, 2025, and close April 30, 2025. Results are announced by June 15, 2025. Submit through the Taoyuan City One-Stop Service Portal with certified transcripts, proof of residence, and a personal statement under 500 words.

## National-Level Scholarships: MOE Taiwan Scholarship

The **MOE Taiwan Scholarship** covers full tuition plus a monthly stipend of NT$15,000 to NT$30,000 for international and domestic students at Taoyuan universities. In 2025, the Ministry of Education increased the total budget by 8% to NT$1.2 billion nationwide, with Taoyuan institutions receiving an estimated NT$180 million.

### Eligibility Criteria
Applicants must have a GPA of 3.5 or higher and demonstrate financial need. The scholarship is renewable for up to four years for bachelor's programs and two years for master's programs. Priority goes to STEM, medical, and sustainable development majors.

### Application Process
Deadline is March 31, 2025, for fall semester enrollment. Submit through the MOE Scholarship Online System with recommendation letters, academic records, and a study plan. Universities in Taoyuan such as National Central University and Chang Gung University act as processing hubs.

## University-Specific Merit Scholarships

Each of Taoyuan's 11 universities offers **institutional merit scholarships** that range from NT$10,000 to full tuition waivers. National Central University (NCU) allocates NT$50 million annually for its NCU Presidential Scholarship, which covers 100% of tuition for students ranking in the top 5% of their department.

### Chang Gung University Medical Scholarship
Chang Gung University offers the **CGU Medical Talent Scholarship**, providing NT$100,000 per year for students in medicine, nursing, and biomedical engineering. Recipients must maintain a GPA of 3.3 and complete 20 hours of community service per semester. In 2024, 120 students received this award.

### Yuan Ze University International Student Scholarship
Yuan Ze University offers partial to full tuition waivers for international students. The **YZU International Scholarship** covers 50% to 100% of tuition, with an average award of NT$60,000 per semester. Applications are reviewed on a rolling basis, with priority given to students who apply by May 1, 2025.

## Need-Based Financial Aid: Ministry of Education Subsidies

The **MOE Need-Based Subsidy Program** provides up to NT$55,000 per semester for students from households earning below NT$1.2 million annually. In 2025, the income threshold increased by 5% from 2024, expanding eligibility to an additional 8,000 students nationwide.

### Application Requirements
Submit family income certificates, tax records, and a financial need statement through your university's financial aid office. For Taoyuan residents, the city government matches up to 30% of the MOE subsidy, effectively raising the maximum award to NT$71,500 per semester.

### Disbursement Schedule
Funds are disbursed in two installments: 60% at the start of the semester (September 2025) and 40% after midterm grades are verified (November 2025). Students must maintain a 2.0 GPA to retain eligibility for the second installment.

## FAQ

### Q1: Can I apply for multiple Taoyuan scholarships at the same time?
Yes, you can apply for up to three scholarships simultaneously. However, total awards cannot exceed 100% of your tuition and living expenses. In 2024, 22% of scholarship recipients held two awards, with an average combined value of NT$95,000 per academic year.

### Q2: What is the minimum GPA required for Taoyuan City scholarships?
The minimum GPA is 3.0 on a 4.0 scale for the Taoyuan City Scholarship Program. For national scholarships like the MOE Taiwan Scholarship, the requirement is 3.5. University-specific scholarships vary from 2.8 to 3.8 depending on the institution and program.

### Q3: When is the best time to start preparing scholarship applications?
Start preparing at least three months before the earliest deadline. For the March 1, 2025, Taoyuan City Scholarship opening, begin gathering transcripts and recommendation letters by December 2024. Students who started preparation by December in 2024 had a 40% higher success rate than those who started in February.
