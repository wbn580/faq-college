Many students assume that merit-based scholarships are only for those with financial need, but in 2025, over $2.6 billion in non-need-based institutional aid is available to U.S. undergraduates, according to the National Association of College and University Business Officers (NACUBO, 2024 Tuition Discounting Study). These awards—often called merit scholarships—are granted based on academic achievement, leadership, talent, or specific affiliations, not family income. The College Board reports that the average merit award at private four-year institutions now exceeds $16,000 per year (College Board, 2024 Trends in College Pricing). For international students, the pool is smaller but still significant: a 2024 survey by the Institute of International Education (IIE) found that 38% of U.S. universities offer some form of merit-based aid to non-citizens. This guide covers the most accessible 2025 scholarship programs that do not require a financial-need demonstration, including university-specific awards, national competitions, and organization-sponsored grants. Each entry includes eligibility criteria, award amounts, and application deadlines.

## University-Specific Merit Scholarships

Many U.S. universities automatically consider all applicants for **merit-based scholarships** without a separate application. These awards reward high GPA, test scores, and extracurricular depth.

### University of Alabama Automatic Merit Awards

The University of Alabama offers the **Presidential Elite Scholarship** covering full tuition plus a $1,000 annual stipend. Eligibility requires a 4.0+ weighted GPA and a 32+ ACT or 1420+ SAT. No separate application is needed if you apply by December 15. The **UA Scholar Award** provides $28,000 over four years for a 3.5+ GPA and 28 ACT or 1310 SAT. International students are eligible with equivalent credentials.

### Arizona State University New American University Scholarship

ASU awards **merit-based scholarships** ranging from $2,500 to $15,500 per year based on GPA and test scores. The highest tier, the President’s Award ($15,500/year), requires a 4.0 GPA and 32 ACT or 1420 SAT. Non-resident and international students qualify. ASU automatically reviews all admitted freshmen.

### University of Southern California Merit Scholarships

USC awards over $100 million annually in merit-based aid. The **Trustee Scholarship** covers full tuition and requires a separate application by December 1. The **Presidential Scholarship** offers half tuition. Both are open to all students regardless of citizenship. USC received over 70,000 applicants in 2024, so early submission is critical.

## National Merit Scholarship Program

The National Merit Scholarship Corporation (NMSC) awards **National Merit Scholarships** to high-scoring PSAT/NMSQT participants. In 2025, approximately 7,600 students will receive awards totaling nearly $30 million.

### Eligibility and Selection

U.S. high school students who take the PSAT/NMSQT in their junior year qualify. The top 1% of scorers in each state become Semifinalists. About 15,000 Semifinalists advance to Finalist status by submitting a detailed application including essays, recommendations, and academic records. Finalists compete for three types of awards: National Merit $2,500 Scholarships (one-time), corporate-sponsored awards (up to $10,000 annually), and college-sponsored awards (full or partial tuition).

### Corporate-Sponsored Awards

**Corporate-sponsored National Merit Scholarships** are available to Finalists who are children of employees of participating companies or residents of specific regions. Award amounts vary but typically range from $2,500 to $10,000 per year. Companies like Boeing, Chevron, and Microsoft participate. These awards do not require financial need.

## Private Organization Scholarships

Numerous private foundations offer **merit-based scholarships** to students regardless of financial background. These often require separate applications and essays.

### Coca-Cola Scholars Program

The Coca-Cola Scholars Foundation awards **$20,000 scholarships** to 150 high school seniors annually. Selection is based on academic excellence, leadership, and community service. The application opens in August and closes in October. Over 100,000 students apply each year, making it highly competitive. No financial information is required.

### Jack Kent Cooke Foundation College Scholarship

The **Jack Kent Cooke Foundation College Scholarship** awards up to $55,000 per year for four years to high-achieving students with financial need. However, the foundation also offers the **Cooke Young Scholars Program** for high-achieving 8th graders, which does not require financial need. The undergraduate scholarship requires demonstrated financial need, but the foundation’s **Graduate Arts Award** and **Transfer Scholarship** have merit-based components.

### Elks National Foundation Most Valuable Student Scholarship

The Elks National Foundation awards **$50,000** (first place), **$30,000** (second), and **$20,000** (third) to 500 high school seniors. Eligibility requires U.S. citizenship, but no financial need is considered. Selection is based on academics, leadership, and community involvement. Applications are due in November.

## International Student Specific Scholarships

International students can access **merit-based scholarships** through several U.S. universities and private organizations.

### University of Miami International Merit Scholarships

The **University of Miami** offers the **Stamps Scholarship** covering full tuition, fees, room, and board for international students. It requires a separate application by November 1. The **President’s Scholarship** offers $25,000 per year. Both are merit-based and do not require financial documentation.

### University of Iowa International Merit Awards

The **University of Iowa** awards the **International Distinguished Scholarship** ($12,000 per year) and the **International Achievement Scholarship** ($8,000 per year) to admitted international freshmen. Eligibility requires a 3.5+ GPA and strong test scores. Automatic consideration with admission application.

### Boston University Trustee Scholarship

Boston University offers the **Trustee Scholarship** to 20 incoming students, including international applicants. It covers full tuition plus a $2,500 annual stipend. Applicants must submit a separate essay and demonstrate exceptional academic or creative achievement. Deadline is December 1.

## Scholarships for Specific Talents or Affiliations

Many scholarships target students with specific talents, backgrounds, or career interests, bypassing financial need entirely.

### National Merit Scholarship for Hispanic Students

The **Hispanic Scholarship Fund** awards merit-based scholarships ranging from $500 to $5,000 to students of Hispanic heritage. Eligibility requires a 3.0+ GPA and U.S. citizenship or permanent residency. Applications open January 1 and close February 15.

### Scholarships for STEM Students

The **Davidson Fellows Scholarship** awards $50,000, $25,000, and $10,000 to students under 18 who complete a significant project in science, technology, engineering, mathematics, music, literature, or philosophy. No financial need is considered. The **Regeneron Science Talent Search** awards $250,000 to the top winner, with 300 finalists receiving at least $2,000 each.

### Scholarships for Arts and Humanities

The **National YoungArts Foundation** awards up to $10,000 to artists aged 15–18 in categories like visual arts, writing, music, and dance. Winners also receive mentorship and performance opportunities. Applications are due in October.

## Application Strategy and Tips

Maximizing **merit-based scholarship** chances requires early planning and strategic application.

### Start Early and Research Thoroughly

Begin researching scholarships in junior year. Use databases like **Fastweb** and **Scholarships.com** to filter by merit and citizenship. Many scholarships have early deadlines (September–December). Create a spreadsheet with deadlines, requirements, and award amounts.

### Tailor Applications to Each Scholarship

Generic applications rarely win. Write specific essays that address each scholarship’s mission. For example, the **Coca-Cola Scholars** program values community impact, so highlight volunteer leadership. The **National Merit** program emphasizes academic rigor and test scores.

### Leverage University Merit Aid

Apply to universities with strong **merit-based aid** programs. Schools like the University of Alabama, Arizona State, and the University of Miami offer generous automatic scholarships. Use the College Board’s **Net Price Calculator** to estimate merit aid at each institution.

## FAQ

### Q1: Can international students apply for National Merit Scholarships?

No. The National Merit Scholarship Program is only open to U.S. citizens and permanent residents who take the PSAT/NMSQT in a U.S. high school. International students studying abroad on U.S. military bases may qualify, but generally, non-citizens cannot participate. However, many U.S. universities offer equivalent merit scholarships for international students, such as the University of Miami Stamps Scholarship and Boston University Trustee Scholarship.

### Q2: What is the average amount of a non-need-based merit scholarship?

The average merit award at private four-year U.S. institutions is approximately $16,000 per year (College Board, 2024 Trends in College Pricing). At public universities, the average is lower, around $4,000 per year for in-state students and $8,000 for out-of-state. Top-tier scholarships like the USC Trustee Scholarship can cover full tuition, which at USC exceeds $68,000 per year.

### Q3: Do I need to submit the FAFSA for merit-based scholarships?

No. The FAFSA (Free Application for Federal Student Aid) is used to determine financial need for need-based aid. Merit-based scholarships do not require the FAFSA. However, some universities may ask for the CSS Profile or institutional forms to verify citizenship or residency status. Always check each scholarship’s requirements—many only ask for transcripts, test scores, and essays.
