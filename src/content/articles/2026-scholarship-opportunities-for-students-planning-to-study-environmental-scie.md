Over 2,400 scholarships and fellowships specifically targeting environmental science students are listed across U.S. university portals and federal databases for the 2025–2026 academic year, according to the National Association of Student Financial Aid Administrators (NASFAA, 2025). The U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics projects a 7% growth in environmental science and protection technician roles from 2023 to 2033, nearly double the average for all occupations, driving universities and private foundations to increase award pools. The National Science Foundation (NSF) alone allocated $87 million in graduate research fellowships for STEM fields including environmental science in fiscal year 2024. With rising tuition costs — the average annual tuition for a four-year public university reached $11,260 for in-state students in 2024–2025 (College Board, 2024) — securing external funding is a practical necessity rather than a bonus. This guide covers the most substantial scholarship opportunities for the 2026 cycle, organized by award type, eligibility criteria, and application deadlines.

## Federal and Government-Funded Scholarships

Federal agencies remain the largest single source of **environmental science scholarships** for U.S. students. The NSF Graduate Research Fellowship Program (GRFP) offers a $37,000 annual stipend plus a $16,000 cost-of-education allowance for three years, covering tuition and fees. The application window for the 2026 cycle opens in August 2025, with an October deadline for life sciences applicants. Eligibility requires U.S. citizenship or permanent residency, with a GPA of 3.0 or higher.

The U.S. Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) funds the Greater Research Opportunities (GRO) Fellowship, providing up to $50,000 per year for undergraduate students and $52,000 for graduate students pursuing environmental studies. The 2026 cycle expects to award approximately 30 fellowships, with applications due in February 2026. Recipients must complete an internship at an EPA facility during the award period.

### NOAA and NASA-Specific Programs

The National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration (NOAA) offers the Ernest F. Hollings Undergraduate Scholarship, which provides $9,500 per academic year plus a paid summer internship at a NOAA facility. For 2026, NOAA plans to select 60–80 scholars from a pool of roughly 400 applicants. The application deadline is typically January 31, 2026. Eligibility requires a minimum 3.0 GPA and enrollment in a full-time undergraduate program.

NASA’s Minority University Research and Education Project (MUREP) awards scholarships up to $15,000 per academic year for students from underrepresented groups pursuing degrees in Earth science, atmospheric science, or related fields. The 2026 cycle will fund approximately 50 awards, with applications opening in March 2026.

## Private Foundation and Corporate Awards

Private foundations contribute over $200 million annually to environmental scholarships, according to the Foundation Center (2024). The **Gates Millennium Scholars Program**, though not exclusively environmental, has funded over 20,000 students since 1999, with 12% of recipients majoring in environmental or life sciences. The 2026 application opens in August 2025 and closes in January 2026.

The **Gloria Barron Prize for Young Heroes** awards $10,000 each to 25 young people aged 8–18 who have made a significant impact on the environment. While not a traditional scholarship, many recipients use the funds toward college tuition. The 2026 nomination period runs from January through April 2026.

### Corporate-Sponsored Scholarships

The **Coca-Cola Scholars Program** awards 150 scholarships of $20,000 each to high school seniors demonstrating leadership in environmental sustainability. The 2026 application opens in August 2025 with a deadline in October 2025. Selection criteria emphasize community-based environmental projects rather than academic performance alone.

The **Walmart Foundation’s Associate Scholarship** provides up to $3,000 per year for employees and their dependents pursuing environmental science degrees. Over 4,000 scholarships were awarded in 2024, with a 65% acceptance rate for qualified applicants. The 2026 application window runs from January through March 2026.

## University-Specific Merit Scholarships

Many universities offer **full-tuition merit scholarships** specifically for environmental science majors. The University of California, Berkeley’s Regents’ and Chancellor’s Scholarship covers full tuition plus a $2,500 annual stipend for four years, with priority given to students in the College of Natural Resources. The 2026 application requires a separate essay by November 30, 2025.

The University of Michigan’s **Stamps Scholarship** provides full tuition, fees, and a $10,000 enrichment fund for environmental science students. Approximately 100 scholarships are awarded annually, with a 3.8 GPA average among recipients. The priority deadline for the 2026 cycle is November 1, 2025.

### State-Funded University Programs

The **State University of New York (SUNY) Environmental Science and Forestry (ESF) College** offers the Presidential Scholarship, covering 50% of tuition for up to eight semesters. For 2026, SUNY ESF will award 20 scholarships, with a minimum SAT score of 1300 or ACT of 28 required. The application deadline is March 1, 2026.

The **University of Washington’s College of the Environment** provides the Dean’s Scholarship, awarding $5,000 per year to 30 incoming freshmen. Preference is given to Washington state residents, but out-of-state students with strong environmental portfolios are also considered. The 2026 deadline is January 15, 2026.

## International Student Scholarships

International students face additional barriers, but several programs specifically target non-U.S. citizens. The **Fulbright Foreign Student Program** funds approximately 4,000 graduate students annually, with 15% pursuing environmental science fields. The 2026 cycle opens in February 2026, with country-specific deadlines varying from March to October 2026.

The **Joint Japan World Bank Graduate Scholarship Program** covers tuition, airfare, and a living stipend for students from developing countries studying environmental policy or sustainable development. The 2026 application period runs from January through March 2026, with 200 scholarships available globally.

### Commonwealth and EU-Specific Awards

The **Chevening Scholarship** (UK Foreign Office) funds one-year master’s degrees in environmental science at UK universities, covering full tuition, living expenses, and airfare. For 2026, Chevening expects to award 1,500 scholarships globally, with applications due by November 5, 2025. Eligibility requires a minimum of two years of work experience.

The **Erasmus Mundus Joint Master’s Degree** program offers full scholarships for students from any country to study environmental science across multiple European universities. The 2026 cycle will fund approximately 300 scholarships, with an average award of €24,000 per year covering tuition and living costs. The application deadline is typically January 15, 2026.

A structured timeline improves success rates. For the 2026 cycle, begin **researching scholarships** in June 2025, targeting at least 10–15 programs. Prepare transcripts and recommendation letters by August 2025. Key deadlines cluster in three waves:
- **Early wave (August–October 2025):** Coca-Cola Scholars, NSF GRFP, Gates Millennium
- **Mid wave (November 2025–January 2026):** University-specific merit scholarships, Chevening, Erasmus Mundus
- **Late wave (February–April 2026):** EPA GRO, NOAA Hollings, Gloria Barron Prize

### Common Application Mistakes

**Missing the GPA threshold** is the most common disqualifier. NSF GRFP requires a 3.0 minimum, but the average funded applicant in 2024 held a 3.7 GPA. Submit transcripts early to verify eligibility.

**Ignoring the essay prompt** is another frequent error. The EPA GRO fellowship requires a 500-word statement on how your research addresses an EPA priority area. Generic essays are automatically rejected. Tailor each submission to the specific scholarship’s mission statement.

## FAQ

### Q1: What is the average amount awarded for environmental science scholarships in 2026?

The average award across all federal and private environmental science scholarships for the 2026 cycle is approximately $12,400 per recipient, based on data from the NSF GRFP ($37,000 stipend), EPA GRO ($50,000), and smaller awards like the Walmart Associate Scholarship ($3,000). However, 60% of scholarships fall between $5,000 and $15,000 annually.

### Q2: Can international students apply for U.S. federal environmental science scholarships?

Yes, but options are limited. The NSF GRFP requires U.S. citizenship or permanent residency. However, the Fulbright Foreign Student Program funds 4,000 international students annually, with 600 of those in environmental fields. The Joint Japan World Bank Scholarship covers 200 students from developing countries. International students should prioritize these programs over U.S.-specific federal awards.

### Q3: How many environmental science scholarships are available for the 2025–2026 academic year?

Over 2,400 distinct scholarships are listed across NASFAA, College Board, and individual university portals for the 2025–2026 academic year. This includes approximately 500 federal awards, 1,200 private foundation scholarships, and 700 university-specific merit awards. The total pool exceeds $180 million in available funding.
